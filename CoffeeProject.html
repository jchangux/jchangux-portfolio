Coffee Enthusiasts

  • Objective: To create a way for people to find specialized coffee shops easier and more efficiently.
  • Problem: User pain points would be searching for a coffee shop is too time consuming and not enough information.
  • My Role: Solo project, so I owned the whole lifecycle from research to final prototyping

Problem Statement:

When people search online for coffee shops in the area, they usually get chains such as Starbucks and Dunkin Donuts. If in the city, chains are around every corner and the multiple locations take up the entire search list. If they want to find a small coffee shop, they have to continuously scroll to find a name they don’t recognize and then research if it’s any good. This could mean going to website, going to yelp from google maps, etc. which is time consuming.

How can we create an app that will be more efficient for the user to find independent coffee shops?

Purpose:

  • Guides you to hidden coffee shop treasures

  • Pay from your phone
Reviews:

  • Some great coffee, some okay coffee

Best Coffee Guide: Founded 2020: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/best-coffee-cafes-guide/id40091695

Purpose:
  • Helps find global speciality coffee shops
  • Shows you coffee shops filtered to your preference
Reviews:
  • Would be better if there were more pictures or a place for travelers to put pictures
  • Making users set up an account is annoying

Competitor Research Analysis:

Pain Points from Users:

  • Not enough pictures
  • Setting up account is a hassle
  • Can’t pinch to zoom on map
  • Can’t favorite shops
  • Can’t share from the app

User Research:

  • Survey was active for 6 days - posted to groups with screening question of if they drink coffee or not
  • 59 people participated; 33 of them drink coffee everyday; 24 of them looking up places before they visit
  • 62% were between the ages 18-24yrs old, 25% were between the ages 25-34yrs old; 12% were over 45 yrs old
  • 5 Main Questions were asked/analysed:
    • What is required for the shop to have to determine visitation?(Type of Coffee, Aesthetics, Affordable Cost, Amount of seating)
    • What method is used to find shops?(Internet search, Walk/Drive By, Word of Mouth)
    • What aspects help decide visitation? (Reviews, Photos, Ratings, Location, Online Menu)
    • Reason for visit? (Enjoy the taste of coffee, Caffeine pick me up, to be social, or looking for best coffee in area)
    • Where they are located(Rurally, Suburbs, Urban) Research Analysis: Based on these results, our main target audience would be an 18-24 year old looking for a specific type of coffee and design. They search for a shop via the internet or walking by and check the reviews and photos to see if they will enjoy the cup of coffee. They most likely live in the city.

User Personas / User Journeys

Context: User is standing on the sidewalk looking for a coffee shop to visit. He/she is looking on their mobile phone.

Journey:

  1. Opens app to Welcome Page
  2. Enters zip code where they want to search
  3. Browses the list of coffee shops that show up
  4. Clicks on the name of the shop for information page

Limitations/Constraints & Retrospective:

Limitations:

A couple limitations I faced during this project included the competition. When I was researching competitors, I couldn’t find an app or website that was exactly what I was looking to do. One of the competitors was highlighted as a mobile coupon to the coffee shops in the area. This has pros and cons to that app that I was not trying to resolve for my users. Another limitation was the participant pool. I was hoping to get more frequent coffee drinkers so that I could analyze those who have experience drinking coffee in all environments. Because only a certain amount within the age range drank coffee every day, it was not a lot of data to go off of.

Retrospective:

I realize I lacked the brainstorming aspect of this project in the user research as well as wireframes. Next time, I will ask strategic questions that will get me better answers to see exactly the pain points users are coming across. This will really help me to understand their problems and come up with ways to solve it. After creating the user personas, I should have spent more time brainstorming multiple ways in which the app will work. Next time, I will be really think out the wireframes and brainstorm them out on paper before going digital.
